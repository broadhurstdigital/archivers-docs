# What Is Explore?

**Explore** is The Archiver's AI research chat — a way to ask questions about your archive and get thoughtful, researched answers backed by the actual documents in your collection.

---

## The plain-English explanation

Think of Explore like having a conversation with a very well-read research assistant who has read every single document in your archive. You ask a question; the assistant answers it by drawing on what's in the documents — quoting them, citing them, and reasoning from them.

*"What was John Smith's relationship with his business partner?"*
*"What do the financial records tell us about the farm's profitability in the 1930s?"*
*"Are there any mentions of the flooding in the village?"*

---

## How it's different from Search

| Search | Explore |
|--------|---------|
| Finds files that match keywords | Answers questions using the content of files |
| Returns a list of results | Returns a researched narrative |
| You read the documents | The AI reads the documents for you |
| Instant | Takes 30–120 seconds |
| No limit on uses | Uses your monthly AI allowance |

---

## What Explore can do

- Answer factual questions about the people, events, and themes in your archive
- Summarise a time period covered by your documents
- Identify who knew whom, who worked together, who had conflicts
- Find connections between documents you might have missed
- Give you a narrative overview of a topic across many documents

## What Explore cannot do

- Access information outside your archive (it only knows what's in your documents)
- Guarantee 100% accuracy — it can misread or misinterpret a document
- Replace your own careful reading of the primary sources

:::tip
Always check Explore's answers against the source documents it cites. The AI is a starting point, not the final word.
:::
