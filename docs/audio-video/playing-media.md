# Playing Audio & Video

The Archiver includes a built-in media player for reviewing audio and video files alongside their AI-generated metadata and transcripts.

---

## Opening the media player

On any accession detail page, click on an audio or video file to open it in the media player. The player opens as a full-screen overlay.

---

## Player controls

### Video files
- Standard video player with play/pause, seek bar, and volume
- Captions overlay when a transcript is available (generated as VTT subtitles)

### Audio files
- Audio playback with a waveform visualisation showing the full audio shape
- Click anywhere on the waveform to jump to that point in the recording

### Playback speed
Both audio and video support adjustable playback speed:
- **0.5x** — half speed (useful for difficult-to-hear recordings)
- **1x** — normal speed
- **1.25x** — slightly faster
- **1.5x** — faster review
- **2x** — double speed

Click the speed button to cycle through options.

---

## Transcript panel

If the file has a transcript (from AI processing or on-demand transcription), it appears in a scrollable panel beside the player.

### Features
- **Auto-scroll** — the transcript follows playback automatically, highlighting the current segment
- **Click to seek** — click any transcript segment to jump to that point in the recording
- **Speaker labels** — if the transcript includes speaker identification, each segment shows who is speaking

### Scroll lock
If you manually scroll the transcript panel, auto-scroll pauses so you can read at your own pace. A "Resume auto-scroll" button appears to re-enable it.

---

## Closing the player

Click the close button or press **Escape** to exit. If you have unsaved transcript edits, you'll be asked to confirm before closing.
